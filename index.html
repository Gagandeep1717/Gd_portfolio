Published research works and papers

Comparative Analysis for Detection of Breast Cancer Using Machine Learning and Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)

C. M. Gagandeep, S. Chithra Shree, Ilham Ahmed, R. Hasini & M. S. Hema

4th International Conference on Computer Vision and Robotics (CVR 2024)

View Publication

Abstract:

Breast cancer remains a major global health issue, where early detection plays a crucial role in improving survival and treatment outcomes. This project focuses on using computer-aided prediction to support doctors in making more accurate decisions and to help patients improve their quality of life. The study compares three different approaches for breast cancer detection—Random Forest, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), and InceptionV3 transfer learning—using medical image data. Texture-based features are extracted from breast images using Local Binary Pattern (LBP) to capture important tissue characteristics. All three models were implemented and evaluated using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The results show that the Random Forest classifier performed the best, achieving an accuracy of 0.9108, followed by the CNN model with 0.8535 accuracy, while InceptionV3 showed lower performance at 0.5924. This highlights how traditional machine-learning models can sometimes outperform deep learning approaches, especially when working with structured, texture-based features.
